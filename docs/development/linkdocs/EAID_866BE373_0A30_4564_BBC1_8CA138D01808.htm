bounded secure managed domain
BSMD
secure peer-to-peer communications between entities (ITS-stations (2.17)) that are themselves capable of being secured and remotely managed; the bounded nature is derived from the requirement for ITS-stations to be able to communicate amongst themselves, i.e. peer-to-peer, as well as with devices that are not secured (referred to as ‘other ITS-stations’), and realizing that to achieve this in a secure manner often requires distribution and storage of security-related material that must be protected within the boundaries of the ITS-stations, leads to the secured nature of the entity, as there is great flexibility to achieve desired communication goals, there is a requirement that this flexibility be managed
